Workshop on

Knowledge-guided Generative AI for Scientific Discovery

ICML 2026 · Details TBA

Introduction

This workshop explores how scientific knowledge can guide Generative AI to solve real scientific problems. Topics include multimodal representation learning, knowledge-grounded generation, data-efficient learning, uncertainty and robustness, and evaluation frameworks aligned with scientific goals.

We welcome participation from researchers and practitioners across AI and scientific domains.

  • Biological Scientists who are interested in applying ML and CV to their research, or who want to learn how to use ML tools to analyze biological images.
  • Machine Learning Researchers who are interested in applying their expertise to biological image data, or who want to learn about the unique challenges and opportunities in this domain.

The workshop will feature keynote talks, paper presentations, and discussions on emerging research at the intersection of GenAI and science. We encourage submissions aligned with the Call For Papers. The workshop will also provide opportunities for networking and collaboration.
